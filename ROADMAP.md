# GeoStamp — ROADMAP (design stage)

This is design intent, not a build order. No v2 code ships in this pass.

## The idea
Stamps are deposits. Each one the writer chooses to place is a small,
declared fact about where a moment happened. Gathered over time, with the
writer's consent, they form a record the writer holds.

## Direction
- **A local, owner-held log.** Stamps accumulate into a store that lives with
  the owner — exportable, deletable, theirs. No server, no account.
- **Mapping material spaces.** Named places ("the room", "la cabina") become
  durable markers, so a stamp can mean a place the writer keeps, not only a
  coordinate.
- **A digital map of the physical world** and of how a person moves and works
  within it — built only from declared deposits, never from scraping, never
  from background collection.

## The founding rule
Self-mapping under self-custody. The map is built by the person it describes,
from what they choose to deposit, and it stays in their keeping. Off remains
the default; nothing is ever recorded unless chosen.
